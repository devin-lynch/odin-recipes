Ramen

Description:

Tonkotsu is a unique style of ramen consisting of an extremely rich, fatty pork broth, fresh noodles, soft-yolk eggs, and tender pork belly that melts in the mouth. It is so popular and special that it could be a dish of its own, not just a ramen style.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound split pig's trotters
  • 2 green onions
  • 1/2 yellow onion, unpeeled, quartered
  • 11-inch piece ginger, unpeeled
  • Sea salt, to taste
  • Soy sauce, to taste
  • 7 ounces fresh ramen noodles
  • 4 slices chashu pork (pork belly or loin cooked in soy sauce and mirin)
  • 1/4 cup enoki mushrooms
  • 2 pieces menma (fermented bamboo shoots
  • 2 pieces nori
  • 1 shoyu tamago (soy sauce egg) or soft boiled egg, halved
  • 1/4 cup green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon coarsely ground sesame seeds
  • Mayu (black garlic oil) or chili oil, to taste
